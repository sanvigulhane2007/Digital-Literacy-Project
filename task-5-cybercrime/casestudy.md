Cybercrime Case Study: Online Payment Fraud

Online payment fraud is a common cybercrime where criminals trick people into transferring money through digital payment platforms. With the increasing use of UPI and online banking, many individuals become targets of such fraud.

In a typical case, a fraudster sends a fake message or call claiming to be from a bank, online store, or payment service. The victim is asked to click a link, scan a QR code, or share an OTP to complete a payment or receive money. Sometimes the fraudster pretends to offer a job, reward, or refund. Once the victim follows the instructions, the fraudster gains access to the victim’s bank account or UPI account and transfers money without permission.

Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not familiar with online security are usually the main targets. Fraudsters often create urgency so that victims act quickly without verifying the request.

The consequences of online payment fraud can be serious. Victims may lose a large amount of money and may also experience stress and loss of trust in digital payment systems. In some cases, recovering the stolen money becomes difficult.

This case study highlights the importance of digital awareness. People should always verify payment requests, avoid sharing OTPs, and never scan unknown QR codes. If a fraud occurs, it should immediately be reported through the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or the helpline number 1930.
